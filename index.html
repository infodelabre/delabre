DÉ.LA.BRÉ

De.la.bre lives in the gap between taste and accident. Between what we seek and what we stumble into.

These objects don't passively occupy space. They interfere. Like Latham's Chew and Spit, they enter the body of daily life, get processed, leave traces.

We source between Rome and Paris. A collision of styles and periods. Objects chosen not for what they represent, but for what they do: graphically, spatially, instinctively.

Patina stays, cracks remain, differences coexist. Design as tension, not resolution.

Sold as found. Honest wear. No manifesto.
